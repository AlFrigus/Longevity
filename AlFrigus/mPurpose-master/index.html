About project

A system for continuous analysis and visualization of data collected from a user's electronic biomedical records using artificial intelligence algorithms.

A bioinformatic algorithm for monitoring the user's physical and mental health. The analyzed data includes indicators of all systems and organs: from DNA and biochemistry to indicators of biorhythms from wearable devices and mental health self-diagnosis questionnaires.

The user will be able to adjust his lifestyle and consciously plan his future consultating an automated system that uses a multi-stage analysis of data collected from thousands of other users, taking into account centuries of theoretical and practical experience collected by medics. The data will also be available to research organizations.

The purposes of the project: studying the nature of aging, improving the quality of life, detecting biological targets, developing regenerative therapy. The smart medical record developed at the BioHackathon will be an interactive personal healthcare module for private use, aimed at integrating into the electronic systems of public and commercial healthcare organizations.

Problem to be solved: at present, such work is done by each ealthcare worker independently: he studies, practices, finds correlations. But this knowledge is incomplete, because the human intellect is not able to store the work experience of a number of patients that is normal in experimental science. The percentage of distribution of some diseases is so small that for the most part only their theoretical study is possible. In addition, science often does not take into account the details of the lifestyle of the researched. Our solution will allow us to study in detail the health of a major part of the urban population in the least invasive way.
